Confluent Cloud Gateway — Cluster Switchover on Azure

What is the Confluent Cloud Gateway? It's a smart proxy that speaks the Kafka protocol and sits in front of your Confluent Cloud clusters. Your apps connect to one address on the Gateway — they never talk to a cluster directly. Behind that single address, the Gateway decides which real cluster to forward traffic to, terminates and re-establishes TLS, and passes your login through untouched. Because the address your apps use never changes, you can move all traffic from the Primary cluster to the DR cluster — a disaster-recovery switchover — just by changing the Gateway's route. The apps never notice.

This page walks through how a client reaches Kafka, what every certificate is for (in plain words), how to deploy it, and how that switchover flips traffic from Primary to DR. Click any box or card for a short "what it is / why we need it" popup.

What "switchover" does

Toggle After failover (DR) up top to watch it. Two moves: move the data, then move the traffic.

1

Pause Primary

Stop writing to Primary; let the copy catch up.

2

Promote the mirror

DR's read-only copy becomes writable. Replication stops. Can't undo.

3

Re-point the gateway

Change the route from cc-primary to cc-dr.

4

Apply & restart

kubectl apply + restart the gateway pod.

5

Clients unaware

Same address, same port — apps never notice.
